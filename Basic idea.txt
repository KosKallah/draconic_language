This Draconic is being created as a usable constructed language for a Pathfinder 2e campaign. Its intended identity is threatening, bestial, ancient, and confident. It must sound natural for dragons rather than for humans, so its design favors throat-driven articulation, low dependence on lip movement, strong guttural resonance, hissing friction, and frequent abrupt consonantal endings. The language should feel predatory, controlled, and old, not chaotic or monstrous in a crude sense.

Its sound design is intentionally restrictive and fully defined by its approved inventory. The phonological core is based on rear articulation dominance, fricative pressure, and hard word closure. Velar, uvular, and glottal sounds are central. Hissing and rasping fricatives are highly valued. Words should usually end in hard consonants rather than vowels, with final vowels kept rare and marked. The official inventory is exhaustive: only the approved consonants, vowels, and orthographic mappings belong to the language unless explicitly added in a later design step.

The writing system for now is a practical Latin orthography meant to be usable at the table while preserving an inhuman phonetic character. Future steps must preserve the current phonological identity and build consistently on it. All later decisions in grammar, phonotactics, morphology, syntax, lexicon, naming, and style must reinforce the same core impression: a dragon-native language that is severe, ancient, confident, and physically shaped by a reptilian throat, jaws, and breath rather than by human lips and soft oral articulation.

Regardless, the language must be relatively easy for (human) players to use and get accustomed to, so excessively alien choices must be avoided in the construction of Draconic.
